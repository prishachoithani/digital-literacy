# Stay Safe Online
### A Checklist for College Students in India

---

## General Digital Safety

- [ ] Use strong, unique passwords for every account — avoid using your name, birthdate, or college roll number
- [ ] Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps
- [ ] Never connect to public Wi-Fi (college cafes, railways, airports) without a VPN for sensitive tasks
- [ ] Regularly review app permissions on your phone — revoke access to camera, mic, and location for apps that do not need it
- [ ] Do not click on links received via unknown SMS, WhatsApp, or email — verify the sender before opening anything
- [ ] Keep your phone's OS and apps updated — security patches fix vulnerabilities that criminals actively exploit

---

## Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] Never post your Aadhaar number, PAN card, or college ID publicly — even partially visible photos can be misused
- [ ] Audit your social media privacy settings every few months — ensure strangers cannot view your location, phone number, or daily schedule

---

## Financial & UPI Safety

- [ ] Remember: receiving money via UPI never requires you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code — if someone insists otherwise, it is fraud
- [ ] Before making any payment on an unfamiliar website or app, verify it is legitimate — look for HTTPS, check reviews, and avoid offers that seem too good to be true

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

Do not wait. Report immediately.

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** cybercrime.gov.in
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 *(available 24/7)*

Early reporting significantly increases the chances of recovering lost funds and stopping the fraudster.

---

*Stay alert. Stay informed. Your digital safety is your responsibility.*
